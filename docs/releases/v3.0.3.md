# v3.0.3 Release Notes

Date: 2026-05-01 (UTC)

## Headline

Two governance additions that you'll feel directly: (1) the active session log now stays compact even on release-heavy weeks — no single closeout entry can crowd out the file you read at every startup; (2) AI replies become more decisive and easier to read — fewer "what do you think?" loops, clearer fact-verification state, no more cryptic `§` codes leading sentences.

## What you'll feel

- **AI replies feel more direct.** When the AI has a recommendation, it states it instead of wrapping it as an open question to push the decision back to you. Choices come capped at 3 with one explicit recommendation backed by evidence.
- **Less cognitive load reading replies.** Sentences lead with what's happening in plain language — internal `§` codes and rule IDs only show up as end-of-line citations for traceability, not as sentence subjects.
- **You can tell when the AI hasn't verified a fact.** Numbers, dates, and citations the AI hasn't confirmed are marked `UNVERIFIED` (distinct from `NA` for genuinely missing values), so you don't get unconfirmed content presented as confirmed.
- **Long projects don't slow down at startup.** Each session's closeout entry is now hard-capped at ≤110 lines including the handoff block. Release-class detail relocates to a separate detail file, so the file your AI reads at every startup stays lean.
- **Fewer over-clarification cycles.** When the AI is genuinely unsure, it's allowed at most 3 hypotheses and 3 questions per round — and once you signal direction (`apply`, `go`, `continue`, or pick an option), it executes without re-confirming.

## What changed

### 1. Reply behavior — five mandatory rules

Five rules now apply to every AI reply (`AGENTS.md` §11a + `INIT.md` mirror, propagating to all installs):

1. **Judgement-first** — state your judgment directly; don't disguise it as an open question
2. **Choice format** — at most 3 options, one explicit recommendation backed by verifiable evidence
3. **Ambiguity handling** — at most 3 hypotheses, at most 3 clarifying questions per round; execute on user signal without re-confirming
4. **Fact verification** — `UNVERIFIED` (unconfirmed) is distinct from `NA` (missing); no presenting unverified content as fact
5. **Plain-language surface text** — `§` codes, rule IDs, and invented terminology may appear as parenthetical citations only, never as sentence subjects in user-facing reply

A new marker line (`MANDATORY REPLY DISCIPLINE`) joins existing `MANDATORY STARTUP` / `MANDATORY WORKFLOW` markers so the rules anchor at the top of the governance file.

### 2. Session log entries — hard cap with overflow valve

`AGENTS.md` §4 was previously soft-targeted at "~20–30 lines per entry". This release adds a **hard cap of ≤110 lines per entry** (counted between `^## YYYY-MM-DD` headers, includes the verbatim handoff block).

Release-class or multi-phase sessions that genuinely need more space relocate detail to:
- `dev/SESSION_STATE_DETAIL.md` for general overflow (created NEW this release)
- `docs/releases/<version>.md` for release-class detail

The active log entry leaves a 1-line reference to the relocated section. Nothing is lost — detail is moved, not deleted.

The previously over-cap entry `Claude_20260425_1017` was refactored from 127 → 72 lines as part of this release, with sandbox QC tables and full release timeline relocated to `dev/SESSION_STATE_DETAIL.md`.

### 3. Output Contract cross-reference fix

`AGENTS.md` §11 previously required "Verification results" but didn't acknowledge the `UNVERIFIED` state introduced by §11a rule 4. Inline citation now added: "Verification results (using §11a rule 4 markers — confirmed facts as facts, unconfirmed as `UNVERIFIED`, distinct from `NA`)".

## Industry standards alignment

The reply behavior rules align with established collaborative-AI patterns: judgement-first reduces decision push-back loops; ≤3 options is the established cognitive cap for choice presentation; the `UNVERIFIED`-vs-`NA` distinction prevents fact-confidence drift documented in API documentation review patterns. The cap of 3 in choice-format / hypotheses / questions per round is derived from real-world AI collaboration retrospectives where higher counts produced choice paralysis and over-clarification cycles.
